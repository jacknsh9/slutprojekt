The m4 GTS

Technology

The BMW M4 GTS was engineered as a track-ready machine that remained street legal. It showcased advanced technology derived directly from motorsport. One of its most innovative features was a water-injection system, which improved engine efficiency and increased power output. This rare technology highlighted BMW M’s engineering creativity.

Aggressive Design & Aerodynamics

The M4 GTS is instantly recognizable thanks to its adjustable rear wing, front splitter, and aggressive aero components. Every element was functional, improving downforce and high-speed stability. Inside, the focus was on performance: lightweight materials, racing seats, and optional roll cage reinforced its track-oriented purpose.

The BMW M4 GTS is powered by a specially enhanced version of BMW’s S55 3.0-litre twin-turbo inline-six, featuring a unique water injection system to boost power and efficiency. The S55 was first introduced in the F80-generation BMW M3 and F82 M4, replacing the previous naturally aspirated V8. It also appeared in models like the M2 Competition and later evolved into higher-performance variants. Over time, the engine received improvements in cooling, tuning, and responsiveness, with the M4 GTS showcasing one of its most advanced and track-focused applications, pushing the limits of the S55 platform.

Performance Benchmark

With 493 horsepower and precision-tuned suspension, the M4 GTS set impressive lap times, including a sub-7:30 Nürburgring lap. It proved that BMW M could still build uncompromising performance machines in the modern era.
